## About this course

This book is an interactive online course on **compressed sensing (CS)** and its application to rapid MR imaging.
It is aimed at students and researchers with a basic understanding of MRI and signal processing.

The course covers:

- The mathematical foundations of compressed sensing — sparsity, incoherence, and nonlinear recovery
- How CS applies to MRI — k-space sampling, Fourier encoding, and clinical tradeoffs
- Interactive reproductions of key figures from Lustig, Donoho & Pauly (2007)

## Reference

This course is based on:

> Lustig M, Donoho D, Pauly JM. **Sparse MRI: The Application of Compressed Sensing for Rapid MR Imaging.**
> *Magnetic Resonance in Medicine*, 58(6):1182–1195, 2007.
